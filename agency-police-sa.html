01Executive Summary

South Australia Police (SAPOL) procured its first documented RPAS fleet in 2013 for use across metropolitan Adelaide and rural SA, with return-to-base and GPS tracking capability and still/video/infra-red camera payloads. SA Police is referenced as a participant in the state/federal police coordination that CASA describes as the National RPAS Working Group, but no current official SAPOL page confirming today's fleet size, platforms or operational scope was found in this research pass — treated as "Highly likely" rather than "Confirmed" pending a direct current-source check.

02Why this matters

Given the age of the only specific, dateable source found (2013) relative to the fast-moving state of Australian police RPAS programs (see Victoria, WA, Queensland), SAPOL's current program scale and capability is a meaningful open research question rather than something to assume from a decade-old article.

Notes

Not to be confused with the SA State Emergency Service (SASES), which separately and explicitly confirms it is CASA-licensed to operate RPAs with trained volunteer pilots (see SASES profile, if populated) — SAPOL and SASES RPAS capability should not be conflated.

04Operational Context

The only specific, dateable primary detail located for SAPOL's RPAS program is a 2013 tender-based news report. More recent, general (non-SAPOL-specific) trade press describes police RPAS programs as existing in "Queensland, South Australia and Western Australia" alongside NSW and Victoria, but without SAPOL-specific detail comparable to the other states profiled in this release.
