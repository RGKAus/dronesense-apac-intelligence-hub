01Executive Summary

A Remotely Piloted Aircraft Operator's Certificate (ReOC) is CASA's organisational certificate that permits a business or agency to trade or operate as a drone service provider "for hire or reward," and unlocks operations beyond what's available under the excluded category. A ReOC is not itself a licence to fly — the organisation must employ or engage RePL-holding remote pilots to actually operate under it. CASA publishes a public, searchable register of current ReOC holders.

02Why this matters

Any public safety agency or critical infrastructure operator planning an in-house (rather than outsourced) RPAS capability beyond simple excluded-category flying needs a ReOC. This is a key differentiator when researching an agency's UAS maturity: whether it holds its own ReOC (in-house capability) versus contracting a third-party ReOC holder (outsourced service) is a directly checkable fact via CASA's public ReOC holder register.

04Operational Context

Before applying for a ReOC, an organisation needs a company Aviation Reference Number (ARN), which the certificate is tracked against. The organisation must nominate a Chief Remote Pilot (CRP) who holds a RePL and passes a CRP assessment, and is responsible for day-to-day sign-off on drone operations. ReOC holders must also develop an operations manual and maintain an operational library. Certain complex operations — for example EVLOS — require the ReOC holder to separately obtain an instrument of approval from CASA in addition to holding the certificate itself. ReOC holders that also want to deliver RePL training to their own or others' pilots must separately apply to add training privileges, which requires nominating a Chief Remote Instructor (CRI) meeting Part 101 MOS standards.
