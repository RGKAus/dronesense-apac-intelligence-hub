01Executive Summary

The AFP is Australia's national policing agency, enforcing Commonwealth criminal law and protecting national interests from crime domestically and overseas. It also delivers community policing in the ACT through ACT Policing (see separate profile) and provides protective security at major airports. No independently confirmed in-house RPAS/drone capability was identified for the AFP as a national agency at time of research — this differs from several state police forces, which have well-documented drone programs.

02Why this matters

As Australia's federal law enforcement agency and the body responsible for aviation security at major airports, the AFP is a relevant stakeholder in counter-drone (C-UAS) policy and airport security even where it may not operate its own drone fleet. It also coordinates with CASA and state police (e.g. the National RPAS Working Group referenced in CASA's own reporting) on drone-related law enforcement policy.

Notes

The AFP is an agency of the Department of Home Affairs (see separate profile), responsible to the Minister for Home Affairs. Since October 2025 the AFP Commissioner is Krissy Barrett. Distinct from ACT Policing, which is the AFP's community policing arm for the ACT.

04Operational Context

The AFP investigates Commonwealth crimes nationally and delivers community policing in the ACT via ACT Policing. Its strategic priorities (terrorism, drug trafficking, transnational crime, cybercrime, child exploitation) are set through Ministerial Direction. Unlike several state police forces, no dedicated AFP RPAS/drone unit or Air Wing equivalent was identified in this research pass — if the AFP does operate drones in a specialist or tactical capacity, this was not confirmed via a public primary source.
