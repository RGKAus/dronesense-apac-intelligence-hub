01Executive Summary

Victoria Police operates one of the largest and most mature police RPAS programs in Australia through its Remotely Piloted Aircraft Systems (RPAS) Unit, part of the Air Wing. As of the most recent official reporting, more than 140 pilots operate more than 130 drones statewide, flying roughly 400 missions per month — a tenfold increase from ~400 flights per year in earlier years. Despite being the last Australian police jurisdiction to begin operational drone use, Victoria Police reached this fleet/pilot milestone earlier than other states.

02Why this matters

Victoria Police is the largest confirmed police RPAS program by pilot and fleet count found in this research pass, spanning missing-persons searches, homicide/clan-lab support, crime-scene documentation, counter-RPAS at major events, and emerging indoor/tactical use — a strong reference case across nearly every public-safety RPAS use case this platform tracks.

Notes

RPAS Unit was used operationally in the manhunt for Desmond Freeman following the shooting of two police officers in Porepunkah. Victoria Police participates in the National RPAS Working Group (state and federal police plus CASA) that secured a 2024 trial exemption for indoor drone flight within 30 metres of people.

04Operational Context

The RPAS Unit trains and maintains currency for remote pilots across the state who hold other primary roles but also fly drones as a secondary capability. It works closely with Missing Persons Unit, Homicide, clandestine-lab units and general intelligence gathering.
