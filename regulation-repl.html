01Executive Summary

The Remote Pilot Licence (RePL) is CASA's individual pilot licence for flying drones/RPA commercially in Australia. It's required to act as a remote pilot for a business holding a ReOC, or to fly an RPA over 25kg (and under 150kg) over your own land. A RePL does not expire and has no minimum age requirement, but the holder must maintain currency and operate under a valid ReOC (except for the narrow "over your own land" exemption). It is issued after completing training with a CASA-approved training provider and passing both a theory exam and a practical flight test.

02Why this matters

The RePL is the baseline credential for any individual pilot in a public safety agency or commercial operator's RPAS program. Agencies evaluating an in-house drone capability need to know that every remote pilot they employ (other than sub-2kg excluded-category operators) must hold a current RePL, and that the agency itself will typically also need a ReOC to operate commercially/officially.

04Operational Context

To obtain a RePL, a candidate enrols with a CASA-approved RePL training provider, completes theory training and passes the CASA-approved theory exam (85% pass mark), completes practical flight training and a competency assessment/flight test, and logs 5 hours flying under the standard operating conditions. If controlled-airspace operation is required, an Aeronautical Radio Operator Certificate (AROC) is also needed (80% pass mark). On successful completion, the training provider — not the pilot — submits the RePL application to CASA. Once issued, the licence can be carried in a digital wallet via the myCASA app. Recognition of prior aviation experience is available through approved training providers for candidates who already hold other aviation qualifications.
