01Executive Summary

NSW Police Force operates RPAS (drones) through its Aviation Command's Air Wing, using pilots holding a Remote Pilot Licence (RePL) under an Operator's Certificate. In February 2026 it launched PolAir-Remote, a drone-in-a-box program (a collaboration between Aviation Command and Technology & Communication Services Command) with an initial deployment at Moree, in addition to remotely piloted units operated from Bankstown Airport.

02Why this matters

NSW Police is a confirmed, named early adopter of drone-in-a-box (dock) technology in Australian policing (PolAir-Remote), making it a directly relevant reference customer and case study for any dock/DFR-style RPAS solution being positioned to Australian police forces.

Notes

PolAir-Remote is a joint initiative between NSW Police's Aviation Command and its Technology & Communication Services Command — worth noting as a model for cross-command RPAS program ownership.

04Operational Context

The Air Wing follows CASA guidelines and internal policy for all RPA operations. Pilots hold a RePL and operate under an Operator's Certificate. The PolAir-Remote drone-in-a-box trial began with two drones on the rooftop of Moree police station (installed January 2026), with two further drones piloted remotely from Bankstown Airport, Sydney.
