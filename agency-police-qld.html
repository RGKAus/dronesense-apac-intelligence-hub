01Executive Summary

The Queensland Police Service (QPS) has used RPAS operationally "for many years in specialist units," per its own Assistant Commissioner, and has run a state-government-funded trial of advanced RPAS in Townsville and Cairns since 2022, adding the Teledyne FLIR SkyRanger R70 alongside an existing DJI Matrice 30 (M30) platform. QPS operates within the Queensland Government Drones Use Policy (QGDUP), which sits under the 2018 Queensland Drones Strategy.

02Why this matters

QPS is a confirmed, named user of advanced military-grade RPAS platforms (FLIR SkyRanger R70) in an active regional policing trial, and operates within a whole-of-government Queensland drone policy framework — useful context for positioning enterprise-grade platforms to Queensland Government generally, not just QPS.

Notes

The Queensland Drones Strategy (2018) and the Queensland Government Drones Use Policy (QGDUP) together frame RPAS use across all Queensland Government agencies, not just QPS — worth reading as a package.

04Operational Context

The Townsville/Cairns trial was announced by the (then) Palaszczuk Government as a 12-month pilot delivering "state-of-the-art" RPAS to police, with QPS RPAS pilots trained in Townsville and Cairns in December 2022. The M30 has already been used operationally (e.g. supporting a Dec 2022 stolen-vehicle recovery in Townsville), while the SkyRanger R70 underwent additional aircraft-familiarisation training before deployment.
