Executive answer

Canada now permits a defined class of lower-risk BVLOS operations without an SFOC-RPAS. A public-safety agency can use this pathway only when the operation remains within the Level 1 Complex limits. The agency must operate under an RPAS Operator Certificate (RPOC), use appropriately declared equipment, and assign pilots holding the Level 1 Complex certificate. Urban or otherwise higher-complexity DFR missions generally remain outside this routine pathway and require an SFOC-RPAS supported by an operational risk assessment.

DFR reality check: Canada has created a routine lower-risk BVLOS pathway, but it is intentionally limited. A typical city-wide DFR deployment may involve populated areas, controlled airspace, aerodrome environments or multiple simultaneous aircraft—conditions that can trigger the SFOC-RPAS pathway.

Lower-risk BVLOS limits

  • Uncontrolled airspace only
  • 400 ft AGL or below
  • More than 5 NM (9.3 km) from the centre of listed aerodromes
  • Small or medium RPA may operate at least 1 km from a populated area
  • Small RPA may operate over a sparsely populated area, or less than 1 km from a populated area, only with the applicable pre-validated declaration
  • Use an RPA and supporting systems with the required safety-assurance declarations
  • Operate under an RPOC with a Level 1 Complex pilot

Level 1 Complex pilot pathway

  1. Be at least 18 years old
  2. Pass the Advanced Operations online exam
  3. Complete at least 20 hours of ground school through a flight school
  4. Pass the Level 1 Complex online exam
  5. Pass a Level 1 Complex flight review
  6. Apply for the Pilot Certificate — Level 1 Complex Operations
  7. Maintain recency and operator-specific competence
DFR questions answered
QuestionCanadian position
Can a police/fire agency launch from a station or dock?Potentially. The launch model itself is not the decisive issue; the operational category, airspace, population exposure, equipment declarations, supervision and procedures determine the approval route.
Can it fly BVLOS without an SFOC?Yes, but only within the lower-risk Level 1 Complex envelope. Many urban DFR concepts will not fit that envelope.
Can it fly over a city to an emergency call?Not as routine lower-risk BVLOS merely because it is public safety. Urban/populated BVLOS generally requires an SFOC-RPAS unless the operation fits a specifically permitted category.
Does emergency-response status remove the rules?No general blanket exemption is identified in the public framework. Government emergency-response organizations are exempt from SFOC application fees, but still need the appropriate operating authority.
Can the agency operate in controlled airspace?Level 1 Complex BVLOS is limited to uncontrolled airspace. Controlled-airspace BVLOS requires the appropriate SFOC route and coordination.
Can one pilot supervise several docked aircraft?More than one RPA operated BVLOS at the same time is listed as a medium-complexity operation requiring an SFOC-RPAS.
Is detect-and-avoid relevant?Yes. The applicable technical declaration and operational-risk controls must address air-risk mitigation. More complex BVLOS applications are assessed through the SORA/ORA framework.
Can the program be scalable?Yes, but scale depends on designing repeatable operations within an RPOC or obtaining an SFOC whose conditions support the intended sites, airspace and mission set.
Practical approval sequence
  1. Define the DFR concept of operations: sites, aircraft, dock, response radius, crew model, airspace and population exposure.
  2. Test each mission against the Level 1 Complex limits.
  3. Where the concept fits, establish the RPOC governance system, train Level 1 Complex pilots and select appropriately declared equipment.
  4. Where it does not fit, identify the SFOC complexity category and complete the required operational risk assessment.
  5. Coordinate airspace, aerodrome, emergency-site, privacy, records, maintenance, cybersecurity and local legal requirements.
  6. Validate procedures through training, exercises and documented assurance before operational deployment.
