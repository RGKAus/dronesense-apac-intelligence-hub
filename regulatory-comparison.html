BVLOS pathways at a glance
US

Waiver / COA

Part 107 waiver for civil operations, or a public aircraft COA with an explicit BVLOS provision. Emergency public-safety operations may use the FAA Special Governmental Interest process.

CA

Level 1 Complex / SFOC

Qualifying lower-risk BVLOS can use Level 1 Complex. Operations outside those limits require an SFOC-RPAS with an operation-specific safety case.

EU

STS / PDRA / SORA

STS-02 can be used by declaration when every condition is met. Otherwise operators use a PDRA or full SORA to obtain an operational authorisation; some LUC holders may self-authorise within privileges.

AU

AusSORA approval

A ReOC holder applies to CASA for BVLOS approval or variation using AusSORA and supporting operational evidence. Pilot BVLOS competency and operation-specific mitigations are required.

NZ

Part 102 certification

The operator obtains or varies a Part 102 certificate. BVLOS privileges, procedures, competency and risk controls are set out in the exposition and accepted by CAA.
