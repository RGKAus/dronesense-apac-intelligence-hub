01Executive Summary

NEMA is the federal government's national disaster management agency, formed 1 September 2022 from the merger of the National Recovery and Resilience Agency and Emergency Management Australia. It sits within the Department of Home Affairs and provides round-the-clock all-hazards monitoring, national coordination, and disaster recovery funding (including the Disaster Ready Fund) — it is a coordination and funding body rather than a frontline operational agency, and no in-house RPAS capability was identified.

02Why this matters

NEMA doesn't operate drones itself, but administers Commonwealth disaster funding (including the Disaster Ready Fund) that state and local agencies can draw on for resilience and preparedness capability — potentially including RPAS-related infrastructure — making it relevant to procurement and funding intelligence even without a direct operational RPAS role.

Notes

Formed by merging the National Recovery and Resilience Agency and Emergency Management Australia; NEMA continues a lineage traced back to the National Disaster Organisation established in 1974.

04Operational Context

NEMA provides round-the-clock all-hazards monitoring and operational coordination for domestic and international emergencies (including supply chain disruptions, critical infrastructure outages and biosecurity risks), works with Recovery Support Officers embedded in communities, and administers the Disaster Ready Fund in collaboration with the states and territories.
