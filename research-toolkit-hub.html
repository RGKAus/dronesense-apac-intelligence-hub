2. Research Methodology

This is the platform's working method for taking a topic from "unresearched" to a page carrying a real Confidence Level. Apply it consistently, for both Australia and New Zealand, so confidence ratings mean the same thing across every page.
  1. Scope the questionWrite the specific question before searching — "does Agency X operate BVLOS" is answerable; "tell me about drones in policing" is not.
  2. Start at the primary sourceRegulator, legislation register, or the organisation's own site first — see the Official Sources Directory above. Treat news coverage and vendor marketing as leads to verify, not as sources themselves.
  3. Cross-check with a second independent sourceA claim confirmed by one press release is "highly likely" at best. Two independent confirmations moves it toward "confirmed".
  4. Record the access dateRegulatory pages change. Note when you accessed a source, not just when it was published, in the page's Sources section.
  5. Rate confidence honestlyUse the six levels below — the same ones used everywhere on the platform. Don't upgrade a rating to make a page look more finished.
  6. Cross-link before you finishAdd the page to every relevant hub and related-page section so it surfaces from more than one entry point.

Confidence ratings, at a glance

ConfirmedStated directly by a primary source, or confirmed independently by two.
Highly likelyOne credible source, not yet independently cross-checked.
Partially verifiedSome elements confirmed; others inferred or unconfirmed.
UnverifiedNot yet researched, or no reliable source found.
HistoricalWas accurate at time of writing; no longer the current state.
SupersededReplaced by a newer regulation, program, or organisation.

Source validation — quick checks

  • Is this the organisation's own site, or a reproduction/aggregator of it?
  • Does the page show a publish or last-updated date, and how old is it?
  • Would a second, independent source (regulator, legislation register, official media release) say the same thing?
  • Is this fact still current, or has it since been superseded — check the regulator's own changelog or news page if one exists?

3. Search Operators

A quick reference for constructing precise queries — copy an example and swap the bracketed terms. Combine operators for the tightest results (e.g. site: + filetype: together).

Operator basics

site: — restrict to one domain

site:casa.gov.au BVLOS

filetype: — target a document type

"[agency name]" annual report filetype:pdf

intitle: — term must be in the page title

intitle:"drone" intitle:"policy" site:govt.nz

inurl: — term must appear in the URL

inurl:tender OR inurl:rfq "unmanned aerial" site:gets.govt.nz

Government procurement searches

Australia — AusTender + state portals

site:tenders.gov.au OR site:tenders.nsw.gov.au OR site:tenders.vic.gov.au "drone" OR "UAS" OR "RPAS"

New Zealand — GETS

site:gets.govt.nz "unmanned aircraft" OR "UAV" OR "drone"

PDF searches

Regulatory guidance PDF

"[regulation name]" filetype:pdf site:casa.gov.au OR site:aviation.govt.nz

Agency annual report PDF

"[agency name]" "annual report" [year] filetype:pdf

CASA searches

Advisory circulars & guidance

site:casa.gov.au "advisory circular" [topic]

RPAS operating categories

site:casa.gov.au intitle:"RPAS" OR intitle:"drone" [category or activity]

4. Research Checklists

Reusable checklists for the five recurring research tasks, plus a quick-scan triage checklist for a fast first pass on any new agency or topic. Work down each list before marking a page's Confidence Level above "unverified".

Agency research

  • Confirmed the agency's exact legal name and jurisdiction?
  • Found its aviation/air-support unit structure, if one exists?
  • Checked its annual report for any drone/UAS mention?
  • Checked the relevant procurement portal for past or open tenders?
  • Identified who owns aviation/innovation decisions, even if inferred?
  • Cross-linked to the mission profiles it plausibly performs?

Company research

  • Classified its actual role (integrator, OEM, dock provider, etc.) — not "drone company"?
  • Confirmed headquarters, founding year, and operating regions from its own site?
  • Listed only publicly confirmed customers, with a source each?
  • Checked for announcements in the last 6 months?
  • Distinguished shipping products from roadmap/marketing claims?
  • Noted funding/ownership if publicly disclosed?

Regulation research

  • Cited the specific clause or section number, not just the instrument name?
  • Checked the regulator's site directly, not a secondary summary?
  • Checked for open consultations or drafts that might supersede this?
  • Noted the issuing body and current status (current/proposed/superseded)?
  • Recorded the access date alongside the publish date?
  • Cross-linked to the agencies and mission profiles this regulation affects?

Technology research

  • Classified maturity as operational today, emerging, or regulatory-dependent?
  • Checked the regulator's treatment of this technology specifically?
  • Listed providers with a source per claim, noting shipping vs roadmap?
  • Identified the key operational constraint (connectivity, cost, approval)?
  • Cross-linked to the mission profiles that depend on it?

Mission profile research

  • Confirmed which agencies actually perform this mission, with sources?
  • Identified the regulatory pathway/approval typically required?
  • Noted typical sensor/payload configuration, sourced rather than assumed?
  • Found a real operational case study, not just a vendor demo?
  • Checked for response-time or outcome data comparing UAS to traditional methods?
  • Cross-linked to the agencies, regulations and technologies involved?

Quick-scan triage checklist

Run this first on any new agency or topic to decide where to dig deeper — one question per theme.

  • Key stakeholders — who owns aviation, innovation or procurement decisions here?
  • Drone programmes — has this organisation publicly confirmed any UAS activity?
  • Procurement opportunities — is there an open tender, panel, or standing offer they could buy against?
  • Legislation — which regulation(s) or Act(s) govern what they can do with UAS?
  • Operational capability — do they already operate aircraft in-house, or contract it out?
  • Technology partners — which vendors, if any, are publicly linked to them?

AI research workflows

A prompt is a starting point, not an answer. Use the workflow below with any prompt from the Prompt Library, in any assistant with web access.
  1. Run the prompt with search enabledPrompts in this library are written to request sources — if a response has none, ask for them explicitly before trusting any claim.
  2. Open every cited source yourselfAI summaries can misstate regulatory detail. Confirm the specific clause, date or figure on the primary source page before it goes into an intelligence page.
  3. Ask a falsifying follow-up"What would prove this wrong?" or "What's the strongest counter-evidence?" surfaces gaps a single confirming prompt won't.
  4. Convert findings, not prose, into the templateExtract facts into Key Facts / Sources / Confidence Level — don't paste AI-generated summary text directly into Executive Summary.
