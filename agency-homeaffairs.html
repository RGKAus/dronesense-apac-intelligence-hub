01Executive Summary

The Department of Home Affairs is the Commonwealth portfolio department overseeing national security, law enforcement, emergency management, immigration and border-related agencies — including the AFP, ABF and NEMA (each separately profiled on this platform). It is not itself an operational or RPAS-operating body, but understanding it as the parent portfolio clarifies reporting lines and helps target procurement/policy research to the right level.

02Why this matters

Home Affairs sets ministerial direction and portfolio-level policy for the AFP, ABF and NEMA — three of the federal agencies with the most public safety and RPAS relevance on this platform. Portfolio Budget Statements published under Home Affairs are a useful source for budget/procurement intelligence spanning these agencies.

Notes

NEMA and the AFP both sit within the Home Affairs portfolio; the ABF is delivered through Home Affairs as its operational border agency. Confirm current Minister and Secretary via a live search when using this page, as ministerial appointments change with government.

04Operational Context

As a portfolio department, Home Affairs' relevance to this platform is primarily as the reporting line and budget-policy context for AFP, ABF and NEMA, rather than as a body to research for direct RPAS operations.
