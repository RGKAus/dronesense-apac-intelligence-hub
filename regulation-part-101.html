01Executive Summary

Part 101 of the Civil Aviation Safety Regulations (CASR) is the single body of Commonwealth legislation governing all unmanned aeronautical activity in Australia — drones/RPA, model aircraft, unmanned balloons, kites, and firework displays. First introduced in December 2001 (in effect from 1 July 2002), it is the legal foundation every other regulation page on this platform (RePL, ReOC, AusSORA) sits underneath. CASA is currently amending Part 101 and its Manual of Standards following a 2021 post-implementation review and 2022 public consultation; some specific instruments are being folded into the regulation itself.

02Why this matters

Any operator, agency, or vendor discussion about "what's legally required to fly a drone in Australia" ultimately traces back to Part 101. It defines who is affected (RPA/drone owners and operators, model aircraft, plus organisations doing law enforcement, surveillance, weather research or airborne telecommunications work), and is the parent regulation for the RePL, ReOC and AusSORA pages on this platform.

04Operational Context

Part 101 sets the top-level rules; the Part 101 Manual of Standards (MOS), issued under regulation 101.028, supplies the detailed technical and operational standards (training/competency, operational procedures) that give the regulation practical effect. Advisory Circulars (e.g. AC 101-01 for licensing/operations, AC 101-06 for AusSORA) provide non-mandatory guidance on how to comply. CASA is presently consolidating several separate legislative instruments into a more streamlined Part 101/MOS structure (proposal PP 2107US), so operators should expect incremental changes over the next release cycle rather than a single "current version."
