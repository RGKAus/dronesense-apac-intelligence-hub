01Executive Summary

CASA is Australia's independent aviation safety regulator and the single authority that governs all remotely piloted aircraft (RPA/drone) operations nationally, from recreational excluded-category flying through to complex BVLOS operations. It issues the licences (RePL), certificates (ReOC) and flight authorisations (via the AusSORA risk framework) that every commercial or public safety RPAS program in Australia depends on. Any agency or company profile on this platform that references a licence, certificate or approval is ultimately referencing a CASA instrument.

02Why this matters

CASA is the single point of regulatory truth for RPAS in Australia — there is no state-level aviation safety regulator. Every public safety agency, critical infrastructure operator or UAS vendor evaluating drone operations in Australia has to work within CASA's framework, so understanding CASA's structure and how it relates to Airservices Australia (airspace) and the states (non-aviation law, e.g. privacy, trespass) is foundational to every other page on this platform.

Notes

CASA was created when the former Civil Aviation Authority was split into two bodies in 1995: CASA (safety regulation, licensing, certification) and Airservices Australia (air traffic control and air navigation services). This split explains why the two organisations appear separately throughout this platform — CASA regulates who may fly and how; Airservices manages the airspace itself.

04Operational Context

CASA operates a tripartite aviation safety structure alongside the Department of Infrastructure and Airservices Australia. For RPAS specifically, CASA licenses individual remote pilots (RePL), certifies operating businesses and agencies (ReOC), registers aircraft, and assesses complex/BVLOS operations through the AusSORA risk-assessment framework (Advisory Circular 101-06). Day-to-day, an agency or company interacts with CASA through myCASA (the online licensing portal), through CASA-approved training providers for RePL/ReOC applications, and directly for BVLOS/complex-operation approvals.
