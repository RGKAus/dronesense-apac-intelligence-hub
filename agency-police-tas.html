01Executive Summary

Tasmania Police confirmed operational drone use in an October 2024 media release describing drones deployed to covertly monitor known hooning hotspots in Southern Tasmania (Rose Bay and Montagu Bay), resulting in charges against two motorists for dangerous driving. Tasmania Police has stated it will continue deploying drones statewide to assist investigations and detect and deter unsafe and anti-social road behaviour.

02Why this matters

Tasmania Police is a confirmed drone user with a specific, named road-policing use case (covert traffic/hooning enforcement) distinct from the search-and-rescue or major-crime use cases more commonly documented in other states — useful for a broader picture of RPAS use-case diversity across Australian police forces.

The confirmed use case (road policing / hooning enforcement) is comparatively unusual among the state police RPAS programs researched for this release — most others emphasise SAR, major-crime or DFR use cases.

04Operational Context

The Oct 2024 deployment resulted in charges against a 17-year-old motorcyclist for reckless riding and an 18-year-old driver for a prolonged burnout, with both vehicles seized/clamped. Tasmania Police's public statement indicates an intent to continue and expand statewide drone deployment for this and other investigative purposes.
