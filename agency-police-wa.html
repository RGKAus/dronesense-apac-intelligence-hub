01Executive Summary

WA Police operates a mature RPAS capability through its Air Wing (RPAS division), and is a recognised leader in Australian police drone innovation — winning the Leadership Award at the Australian Association for Uncrewed Systems industry awards. WA Police has launched a "Drone in a Box" operational trial, and — notably — negotiated, on behalf of Australian law enforcement agencies generally, regulatory exemptions from CASA in December 2025 allowing police drones to operate BVLOS and over populous areas.

02Why this matters

WA Police's December 2025 CASA exemption is a nationally significant regulatory milestone: it was negotiated on behalf of Australian law enforcement broadly, not just WA, and directly affects the BVLOS/AusSORA pathway available to every other state police force on this platform — this is one of the highest-value regulatory intelligence findings from Release 2.

Notes

All WA Police RPAS pilots must maintain currency against minimum flight-time requirements and pass an annual proficiency knowledge check plus practical testing; the RPAS team separately undertakes proficiency checks on flight ops, indoor flying and payload delivery, tracked through a safety management system.

04Operational Context

WA Police's BVLOS/populous-area exemption (negotiated with CASA in December 2025) was secured on behalf of Australian law enforcement agencies as a group, via the National RPAS Working Group (state and federal police plus CASA) — the same body responsible for the 2024 indoor-flying trial exemption referenced on the Victoria Police profile.
