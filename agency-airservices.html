01Executive Summary

Airservices Australia is the government-owned statutory authority and Australia's principal civil air navigation service provider (ANSP), responsible for air traffic control, aeronautical information, radio navigation aids, and Aviation Rescue Fire Fighting Services (ARFFS) at major airports. It manages Australian-administered airspace — the airspace RPAS operators must be deconflicted from — making it a key regulatory-adjacent stakeholder for any BVLOS or controlled-airspace drone operation, distinct from CASA's role as safety regulator.

02Why this matters

Airservices is not a drone operator, but it is the organisation any BVLOS or controlled-airspace RPAS operation must ultimately be deconflicted against — relevant to UTM/Flight Information Management System (FIMS) discussions and to any agency's BVLOS approval pathway (see BVLOS & AusSORA).

Notes

Formed alongside CASA in the 1995 split of the former Civil Aviation Authority; CASA regulates safety, Airservices manages the airspace itself. Aviation search and rescue coordination was transferred from Airservices to AMSA in 1997.

04Operational Context

Airservices operates under the Air Services Act 1995 and is fully funded by industry through a five-year pricing agreement, with priorities set by the Minister for Infrastructure and Transport via a public Statement of Expectations. It works alongside CASA, the ATSB, the Bureau of Meteorology and Defence in Australia's collaborative air traffic management arrangements.
