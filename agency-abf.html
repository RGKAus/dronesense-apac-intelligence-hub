01Executive Summary

The ABF is the operational border and maritime law enforcement agency within the Department of Home Affairs. It has publicly confirmed, on its own website, operational use of RPAS (drones) for maritime surveillance in northern Australia — including Operation LUNAR (Northern Territory) and integration into Operation LEEDSTRUM in the Kimberley Marine Park, WA — to counter illegal foreign fishing. This is one of the strongest-confirmed federal RPAS use cases found in this research pass.

02Why this matters

The ABF is a confirmed federal RPAS operator with a specific, named use case (maritime/border surveillance in remote, hard-to-access terrain) and is separately procuring a large crewed aerial surveillance capability (a new 11-aircraft contract awarded to Metrea Australia, effective from 1 January 2028) — both of direct relevance to companies offering RPAS or broader aerial ISR capability into the public safety and border security market.

Notes

RPAS use sits within a broader Maritime Border Command surveillance mix (aerial, patrol boats, land patrols, community/Indigenous ranger partnerships) — drones are described as one complementary layer, not a replacement for crewed assets.

04Operational Context

ABF's RPAS rollout is part of a broader program across northern Australia, run alongside Maritime Border Command's other surveillance assets. Separately, the ABF (via the Department of Home Affairs) has engaged Metrea Australia Pty Ltd for a new civil maritime surveillance contract — a fleet of 11 crewed aircraft (including 3 Bombardier Global 6500 jets) providing 24/7 short- and long-range aerial surveillance from 1 January 2028, following a competitive procurement process announced 22 May 2026.
